Road Safety Enforcement in Australia:
Drug Driving Analysis

This website presents an analysis of drug-driving enforcement across Australia between year 2023 to 2024. It explores key insights such as:

  • The number of positive drug tests
  • Trends in charges and arrests over time
  • The demographic groups most affected
  • Jurisdictions with the highest number of fines
  • The types of drugs detected in each jurisdiction

It aims to provide a clear understanding of drug-driving patterns and support data-driven discussions on road safety enforcement.

In which quarter of the year was the highest number of positive drug tests recorded?

Tests Conducted vs Positive Tests Over Time

From 2023 to 2024, the frequency of drug test conducted and positive drug test results fluctuated significantly across the quarters, rather than following a steady trend.

During the period from 2023 to 2024, the quarters with the highest volume of activity were:

  • Drug Tests Conducted: Q1 2024
  • Positive Tests: Q4 2023

* Q1 (Jan–Mar), Q2 (Apr–Jun), Q3 (Jul–Sep), and Q4 (Oct–Dec) represent the first, second, third, and fourth quarters of the year, respectively.

What trend can you observe in the number of charges and arrests over time?
Which age group has the highest number of charges and arrest?

Charges and Arrests Analysis

Between 2023 and 2024, enforcement actions, including charges and arrests, increased steadily. The 26–39 age group experienced the highest level of enforcement, while those aged 0–16 recorded the fewest charges and individuals aged 65 and over recorded the fewest arrests.

* Charges: Offences where formal legal charges were laid, including a Court Attendance Notice.

* Arrests: Offences where a person was taken into custody by police.

Among all jurisdictions, which has the highest number of fines?

Drug Fines by Jurisdiction

Drug-driving enforcement is not uniform across Australia, with significant disparities in the volume of fines issued between regions. New South Wales (NSW) records the highest number of penalties, followed by South Australia (SA) and the Northern Territory (NT). In contrast, the remaining jurisdictions report zero fines during the 2023–2024 period.

* Fines: Offences that resulted in a monetary penalty, including infringement notices.

Which drug type appears most frequently across jurisdictions?

Drug Types by Jurisdiction

Heatmap frequency analysis shows that New South Wales (NSW) recorded the highest number of detections across nearly all substance categories, particularly cannabis and amphetamine. In contrast, Victoria (VIC), South Australia (SA), and the Australian Capital Territory (ACT) reported moderate to low detection levels for certain drugs, while Queensland (QLD), Tasmania (TAS), and Western Australia (WA) recorded no detections across all drug types.

* The "Number" represent the count of "Yes" responses for each respective drug type in the corresponding jurisdiction, according to the dataset.

* "Other Drugs” includes all detected substances not specific classified, as well as detections involving unknown drug types.
